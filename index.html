Income and Poverty Data Reveal a Divided King County

A deep dive into the socioeconomic fissures within Washington State's most populous county.

Interactive: Median Household Income by Census Tract

Scroll to read the story

Median household income varies widely across King County, revealing a clear geographic divide between affluent and lower-income communities. Census tract data show clusters of higher-income neighborhoods concentrated on the Eastside, while southern areas report substantially lower median incomes.

City-level poverty data reinforce this divide. Communities with lower median incomes also tend to have higher poverty rates, in some cases exceeding 13 percent. In contrast, higher-income Eastside cities generally post poverty rates in the single digits.

Because King County is home to more than 2.2 million residents, these disparities affect large numbers of households. In lower-income cities, thousands of residents fall below the federal poverty line, underscoring how income inequality translates into concentrated economic vulnerability.

Taken together, the income map and poverty rates suggest that economic differences in King County are not evenly distributed but spatially clustered, with affluent and economically vulnerable communities often separated by only a short distance.
